About our Products

 

What are marzipops?

Marzipops are delicious playful lollipops hand‐made from marzipan.

 

What is marzipan?

Marzipan is a luscious combination of sugar, ground almonds and water. Preservative is added to increase shelf‐life and we use food color to create a vivid appearance.

 

Where do you get your marzipan?

We use marzipan made by Mandelin, a superb quality almond grower based in San Luis Obispo, California.

 

How big are the marzipops?

Each marzipop is approximately 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter, 3/8" thick, and cast onto 4.5" white lollipop sticks. They are approximately 5" to 6" tall. We offer options to customize your marzipops with longer sticks, with colored plastic sticks, or with no sticks at all.

 

How are marzipops packaged?

Each pop is individually wrapped and sealed in a clear plastic wrapper with a twist‐tie, and then each wrapper is labeled. We offer options to customize your packaging with ribbons and printed tags.

 

Are there any products without sticks?

All our lollipops can be ordered without the sticks and presented as candies. We also offer marzipan bites, small pieces of marzipan candy in selected designs that come gift‐boxed by the dozen.

 

Are your figures kosher / gluten‐free / vegan? What about allergens?

Marzipan is made from almonds and contains nuts. Our products are vegan and gluten‐free BUT we do not work in a vegan or gluten‐free facility, so people with a severe allergy should not order from us. Currently our products are not certified kosher.

 

How do I eat my marzipops?

You can eat them any way you want! You can lick them like a traditional lollipop, or bite into them like a piece of candy. You can eat them at room temperature or pop them into the refrigerator for a super refreshing treat!

 

How should I store my marzipops?

The pops should be stored in airtight packaging and placed in a cool, dry location.

 

How long will my marzipops last?

Marzipops will stay fresh for 1‐2 months if stored properly. Afterwards they will start to harden.
